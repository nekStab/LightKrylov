public subroutine cg(A, b, x, info, preconditioner, options)

Implements the classic Conjugate Gradient (CG) algorithm for solving symmetric positive definite linear systems of equations, .

Advantages

  • Well-suited for large, sparse, symmetric positive definite (SPD) matrices.
  • Memory-efficient, requiring storage for only a few vectors in comparison to GMRES.
  • Under exact arithmetic, finds the exact solution within 'n' iterations for an n-dimensional SPD matrix.

Limitations

  • Applicability restricted to SPD matrices.
  • Might take a large number of iterations for ill-conditioned problems.

References

  • Hestenes, M. R., and Stiefel, E. (1952). "Methods of Conjugate Gradients for Solving Linear Systems," Journal of Research of the National Bureau of Standards, 49(6), 409–436.
